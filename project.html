PROJECT: USING DATA ANALYSIS IN FORMULA 1

AIMS

Strategy is a key component for success in a Formula 1 race. Whether calling to pit or choosing the tires to fit, strategist aim to optimize the performance of their team to ensure that the car finishes in the highest position possible. Before, during and after races, Formula 1 aficionados use data to understand teams’ decisions and try to predict their strategy. Using Formula 1 as an illustration, the aim of this project is to show how Data science can applied in topics outside the world of economics such as sports. To that end, the 2022 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix will be illustrated and analysed below. More specifically, Charles Leclerc and Max Verstappen’s performance will be studied to provide an insight into the world of Formula 1, the events of the 2022 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix and the media surrounding.

The data used in this project comes from FastF1. FastF1 is python package that provides access to F1 lap timing, car telemetry and position, tyre data, weather data, event schedules and session results. Data was extracted from the library using Google Collab python notebook. The Data was further complemented by scraping motorsports websites.

The Race Strategy for the Abu Dhabi 2022 GP was created using this notebook. By changing Dates or Race Tracks on the python notebooks, a new Race Strategy can easily be created. Tyre type and pit stops are seen as the core of Race Strategy. The use of hard tyres by three drivers stands out. Most teams also seem to favour starting with soft tyres. Furthermore, pit stops are mostly close together, with some outliers.

The notebook for the 2022 Teammate Qualifying Battle can be found here.

The Racepace figures were created from this same notebook. The teams represented are Red Bull, Ferrari and Mercedes.

The notebook for the Telemetry Comparison can be found here. Verstappen (first) and Leclerc’s (second) fastest race lap are compared. Leclerc maintains a higher speed and the throttle longer throughout the race.

The 2022 AD Corner Analysis was created using this notebook. Despite Verstappen’s ability to stay in full throttle longer this time, Leclerc manages to get through this corner sequence faster (9.86km/h faster). Charles Leclerc finished second in Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, beating Sergio Perez. Charles praised Ferrari’s one-stop strategy executed to “perfection”. Red Bull similarly followed a one-stop strategy, allowing Max Verstappen to Stay in first place despite Leclerc’s performance.

The use of the FastF1 API allows this project to be easily replicated and automation. That said, the nature of the data makes it hard to manipulate, restricting this project to visual representation of data. As Formula 1 is a sport with limited number of participants and races, the data collected was too small. Furthermore, this project’s focus, human performance, added additional restrictions. All attempts to build a dataset for machine learning can be found here, with any notebooks used. The small amount of data available and the nature of the data resulted in inconclusive and insignificant results. While machine learning can be very helpful for understanding components (such as tyre degradation), it can provided be little information on human performance in small samples.

CONCLUSION

The use of data visualisation provides us with a better understanding of Formula 1 races. From illustrating race strategies to comparing fastest laps, graphs and visual representation are becoming essential supports. With the introduction of a budget cap for R&D expenditure this year (to level the playing field), strategy is becoming more popular and critical. While teams and drivers always analysed data, representing data from races is now crucial insight for viewers as it allows them to better understand the sport and its challenges. Coming at time of insecurity for Ferrari due speculation over the future of team principal, Charles Leclerc was praised for his performance during the 2022 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix. This project showcased his performance during the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix and provided insight on his competitors. From the data visualisation, I hope an insight on his performance was provided and explanation on the news surrounding this Grand Prix. While data science was purely used for visualisation in this project, machine learning plays an increasing important role in the sport. As teams and strategist focus on models to assist their drivers performance, fans will have to weight for a more robust API.
